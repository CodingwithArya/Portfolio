About Me

Hi! I’m Arya, an undergraduate student majoring in computer science at UW-Seattle.
I’m passionate about working on tech that makes a difference.
In high school, I spent a year doing research and developing machine learning (ML) models to help tackle Seattle’s homelessness crisis. My models identified where resources should be allocated to maximize the number of people transitioning out of homelessness.
In college, I’ve continued similar research, applying ML to make a difference, under the Disaster Data Science Lab. My work has focused on applying ML techniques to predict bridge characteristics to enable realistic emergency prioritization planning during natural disasters under the U.S. Dept. of Transportation. I am also developing the H2azaRDS Tool under the WA Dept. of Health to support hazard risk assessment in local health jurisdictions.
Outside of my research, I’m deeply passionate about outreach, specifically within education. At UW, I am leading Women in Computing’s Outreach committee, where we run beginner-friendly CS workshops for K-12 students. Last year, I was also a teacher’s assistant for the Interactive Intelligence (I2) Club’s Deep Learning/Neuroscience course. This year, I’m excited to be co-leading the course to help educate more students on machine/deep/reinforcement learning, computer vision, and neuroscience! LINK THIS. I have also co-led I2’s Kaggle Competition Club, where I directed weekly predictive ML workshops on Linear/Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost models in Python.
In the future, I would love to leverage my ML or Data Science expertise as a ML Engineer, SWE, or Data Scientist, which focuses on addressing significant challenges across any domain.
